pune food tour





Given a list of locations, the challenge of finding the shortest possible route that visits each location and returns to the original location is called the Travelling Salesman Problem. There are many algorithms that attempt to solve this problem. One of the easier ones is a greedy algorithm, which makes the locally optimum decision at each point in the hope of finding a global optimum. I made a list of my favorite places to eat at in Pune, and implemented a greedy algorithm to solve this particular case of the TSP. The output (all distances taken as the crow flies) is described by the polygon on the map above, and the complete route is written out below.
